Current work

August 2025 - Present

Testing the limits of Phase-Coherent GW Sky Maps

Working on an extension of the MIMOSIS (see below) pipeline. The goal of this extension is to allow for hotspot searches using Bayesian analysis on the data, as well as comparing stochastic graviational wave background models to models of several point sources of gravitational waves to determine best fit. Currently all of this is conducted on simulated pulsar sets with simulated gravitational wave signal.
Advisors: Dr. Gosia Curyło, Dr. Eric Thrane, and Dr. Paul Lasky

Prior research

June 2025 - April 2026

A Comprehensive Framework for Phase-Coherent Mapping of the Gravitational-Wave Sky with Pulsar Timing Arrays (MIMOSIS)

Abstract: We present a practical implementation of a phase-coherent mapping technique for pulsar timing arrays that resolves the full complex polarisation state of the gravitational-wave sky as a function of direction and frequency. Unlike standard cross-correlation methods, this approach preserves the amplitude, phase, and polarisation of the signal in every sky pixel. The resulting maps constitute a compact, minimally processed summary of the data from which all subsequent analyses -- characterisation of a stochastic background, searches for anisotropy, and identification of individual sources -- can be derived within a single unified framework. Our implementation is fully compatible with established pulsar timing data analysis methods. We validate the framework through a series of realistic simulations with varying array configurations, noise properties, and signal types. We demonstrate robust recovery of source amplitudes and sky locations across different scenarios, and discuss the impact of polarisation leakage, noise, and direction-dependent array sensitivity on the recovery of astrophysical signals.
Advisors: Dr. Gosia Curyło, Dr. Eric Thrane, and Dr. Paul Lasky
